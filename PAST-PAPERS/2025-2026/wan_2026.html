Question 19 • Loop Prevention Section B
Spanning Tree Protocol (STP) State Operations

Investigate convergence states and structural loop mitigation metrics within high-availability redundant LAN designs.

STP Engineering Breakdown:
A) Impact of Listening and Learning Stages

Listening Stage (15 Seconds): The physical switch interface drops incoming host payload traffic entirely while evaluating structural Bridge Protocol Data Units (BPDUs) to locate alternative redundant tracking paths and root switch mappings. This prevents frame loops from forming while the topology settles.

Learning Stage (15 Seconds): The switch continues to discard host payload traffic but scans the source addresses of incoming frames to populate its MAC address table. Together, these stages introduce a 30-second convergence delay before transitioning an interface from blocked to active forwarding mode.

B) Impact of STP Timers on Network Stability
  • Hello Time (2s): Dictates the interval at which the Root Bridge broadcasts configuration BPDUs to maintain topology state.
  • Max Age (20s): The duration a switch stores neighbor BPDU parameters before declaring them invalid if communications fail.
  • Forward Delay (15s): The operational timer tracking execution steps for both the Listening and Learning phases individually.
