D. OPEN QUESTIONS — 35 Marks

1. CCTV Terms (5 Marks)

  1. CCTV System: A closed-circuit monitoring system used for surveillance and security.
  2. IP Camera: A digital camera that transmits video over a network using an IP address.
  3. DVR: A recording device used for analog CCTV systems using coaxial cables.
  4. NVR: A digital recorder for IP cameras that receives video over Ethernet networks.
  5. Site Survey: A physical inspection of a location to determine installation requirements.

2. Key activities during a physical site survey (6 Marks)

  • Assess building layout
  • Identify camera positions
  • Determine field of view
  • Identify cable routes
  • Assess lighting conditions
  • Determine mounting surfaces / equipment placement

3. Five tools used in CCTV installation (5 Marks)

Any five of the following (½ mark tool + ½ mark use):

  • Drill – drilling holes for mounts
  • Screwdriver – tightening screws
  • Crimping tool – making network cable ends
  • Cable tester – testing connectivity
  • Hammer – fixing mounts or trunking
  • Pliers – cutting or bending wires
  • Measuring tape – measuring distances

4. Differentiate Analog CCTV and IP CCTV (4 Marks)

Analog CCTV

  • Uses coaxial cable
  • Uses DVR for recording

IP CCTV

  • Uses network cables (Ethernet)
  • Uses NVR and network switches

5. Importance of designing a CCTV system diagram (5 Marks)

  • Helps plan camera locations
  • Determines cable routes
  • Avoids installation mistakes
  • Helps estimate materials
  • Serves as reference for maintenance

6. Trunking types & underground usage (4 Marks)

a) Two types (any two): Visible trunking, Built-in, Semi-built-in, Waterproof, Underground

b) Why underground trunking? Protects cables from weather and prevents vandalism/theft.

7. Cost estimation components (4 Marks)

  • Item/specification list
  • Item unit cost
  • Total project cost (quantity × cost)

8. Why labeling is important (2 Marks)

  • Helps identify cables and cameras easily
  • Makes troubleshooting faster

9. Difference: equipment, materials, tools (2 Marks)

Equipment: electronic devices used in the system (cameras, NVR, switch).
Materials: installation accessories (cable ties, pipes, trunks).
Tools: instruments used to perform installation (drill, screwdriver).

SECTION B — Answer only ONE question (15 Marks)

QUESTION 1 — Site installation (15 Marks)

a) Site survey steps (3 Marks)

  • Inspect the building layout
  • Identify suitable camera positions and angles
  • Identify cable routes and equipment locations

b) Design CCTV system diagram (3 Marks)

  • Draw building layout or import plan
  • Place camera icons with labels
  • Add cable paths, recorder, switch, and power supply locations

c) Materials & tools for trunking (3 Marks)

  • Materials: cable ties, trunking pipes, screws
  • Tools: drill, screwdriver, measuring tape

d) Install cameras & connect to DVR/NVR (3 Marks)

  • Mount cameras on walls/ceilings
  • Run cables through trunking
  • Connect cables to DVR/NVR and power system

e) Test CCTV system (3 Marks)

  • Check video feed from each camera
  • Verify recording on DVR/NVR
  • Test night vision and camera angles

QUESTION 2 — 12 IP cameras to NVR (15 Marks)

a) Plan & estimate cost (3 Marks)

  • List all required items (NVR, 12 IP cameras, cables, POE switch)
  • Check item specifications (resolution, storage)
  • Calculate total cost (quantity × price)

b) Select equipment (3 Marks)

  • Choose cameras with required resolution and night vision
  • Select NVR compatible with 12+ channels and storage
  • Choose POE switch based on power budget and port count

c) Waterproof trunking steps (3 Marks)

  • Use waterproof piping and conduit
  • Seal joints with waterproof tape or silicone
  • Route trunking to protect cables from rain

d) Connect IP cameras via switch (3 Marks)

  • Connect all cameras to POE switch ports
  • Connect the switch to the NVR
  • Ensure NVR and cameras are on same network/VLAN

e) Configure & test NVR (3 Marks)

  • Assign IP addresses to cameras (static or DHCP reservations)
  • Add cameras to NVR using ONVIF or manufacturer method
  • Check live view and recording for all 12 cameras
