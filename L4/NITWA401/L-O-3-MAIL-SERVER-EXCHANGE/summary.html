Email Systems & Services Notes

1) Microsoft Exchange Server

What it is:

A mail server system used by organizations to manage email, calendars, contacts, and tasks.

Key Points:
  • Installed on company servers (on-premises) or used as Exchange Online (cloud).
  • Manages user mailboxes.
  • Handles email sending and receiving.
  • Provides enterprise-level security and control.
  • Used mostly by businesses and institutions.
Exchange is the engine that runs the email system in many companies.

2) Microsoft Outlook

What it is:

An email application (client) used to access and manage emails.

Key Points:
  • Installed on a computer or used via web (Outlook Web).
  • Connects to Exchange, Microsoft 365, Gmail, or other email servers.
  • Lets you read/send emails, manage calendar, tasks, and contacts.
  • Does NOT store company email systems itself (unless using local files).
Outlook is the app you use to open and manage your email.

3) Microsoft 365

What it is:

A cloud-based subscription service that includes email, Office apps, and collaboration tools.

Key Points:
  • Includes Exchange Online (cloud email server).
  • Includes Outlook, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, and OneDrive.
  • Hosted in the cloud.
  • Monthly or yearly subscription.
  • Designed for businesses, schools, and individuals.
Microsoft 365 is a complete online productivity package, and Exchange Online is part of it.

4) Gmail

What it is:

A free web-based email service provided by Google.

Key Points:
  • Works through web browser or Gmail app.
  • Uses Google’s mail servers.
  • Includes Google Workspace (business version).
  • Cloud-based.
  • Popular for personal and business use.
Gmail is Google’s email service, similar to Outlook email but hosted by Google.
