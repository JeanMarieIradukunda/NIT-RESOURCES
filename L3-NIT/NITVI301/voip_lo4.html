DESCRIPTION OF VOIP SYSTEM INFORMATION
1. Types of Information in a VoIP System
  • Voice
    • The primary type of information in VoIP.
    • Human speech is converted into digital signals and transmitted through the internet.
    • Used for phone calls between users on computers, IP phones, or smartphones.
  • Video
    • Allows video calls or video conferencing between users.
    • Voice and moving images are transmitted together in real time.
    • Commonly used in online meetings and remote communication.
  • Picture (Images)
    • Users can send or share still images during communication.
    • Useful for presentations, demonstrations, or sharing visual information.
  • Text
    • Includes instant messaging, chat, and written communication during calls.
    • Often integrated with VoIP platforms to allow users to send messages while talking.
  • Email
    • VoIP systems may integrate with email services.
    • Examples include voicemail sent to email or sending files and messages through email connected to the VoIP platform.
2. Description of backup types

Backup types refer to different methods used to copy and store data so it can be restored if the original data is lost, damaged, or deleted.

  1. Full Backup
    • A full backup copies all data and files from a system.
    • Advantages: Easy and fast to restore.
    • Disadvantages: Takes more storage space and more time to create.
  2. Incremental Backup
    • Copies only data changed since the last backup (full or incremental).
    • Advantages: Faster and uses less storage space.
    • Disadvantages: Restoration can take longer because multiple backups may be needed.
  3. Differential Backup
    • Copies all data that has changed since the last full backup.
    • Advantages: Faster restoration than incremental backups.
    • Disadvantages: Requires more storage space than incremental backups.
  4. Mirror Backup
    • Creates an exact copy of the source data.
    • If a file is deleted in the original location, it is also deleted in the mirror backup.
    • Advantages: Provides a real-time duplicate of data.
    • Disadvantages: Does not keep previous versions of files.
  5. Cloud Backup
    • Data is backed up to online cloud storage servers through the internet.
    • Advantages: Accessible from anywhere and protects against local hardware failure.
    • Disadvantages: Requires internet access and may involve subscription costs.
3. Use of Data backup Techniques/Technologies
  • Removable Media
    • Devices such as USB flash drives, CDs, DVDs, or memory cards used to store backup copies of data.
  • Redundancy
    • Keeping duplicate copies of data or systems.
  • External Hard Drive
    • External hard disk for large backups.
  • Backup Software
    • Programs to automatically copy and manage backups.
  • Cloud Backup
    • Data stored on remote servers via the internet.
MONITORING OF VOIP SYSTEM
1. Description of Monitoring Metrics (VoIP Network Performance)
  • Jitter – Variation in the time delay between voice packets.
  • Latency – Delay for voice packets to travel from sender to receiver.
  • Packet Loss – Missing data packets during transmission.
  • Quality of Service (QoS) for Voice – Techniques to prioritize voice traffic.
2. Description of Monitoring Methodology
  • Data Collection – Gathering raw information from devices and networks.
  • Data Aggregation – Organizing and summarizing data for analysis.
  • Data Analysis – Examining aggregated data to identify patterns and issues.
3. Use of VoIP System Monitoring Tools/Software
  • Real-Time Performance Monitoring – Track latency, jitter, packet loss, call quality.
  • Quality of Service (QoS) Assurance – Ensure voice traffic priority.
  • Troubleshooting and Diagnostics – Logs and reports for call issues.
  • Historical Data and Trend Analysis – Store performance data over time.
  • Alerts and Notifications – Automatic alerts for thresholds.
  • Reporting and Compliance – Reports for audits, billing, and regulatory compliance.
INTERPRETATION OF VOIP CALL LOGS

VoIP call logs provide detailed records of telephone activity within a VoIP system.

Description of VoIP Call Log Metrics
  • Date and Time of a Telephone Call – Shows when a call was initiated.
  • Call Duration (in minutes) – Indicates how long the call lasted.
  • Information on Source and Destination Telephone Numbers – Identifies caller and receiver.
  • Type of Phone Call – Toll-free, inbound, or outbound.
  • Cost of a Call (Per Minute Rate) – Associated per-minute cost.
  • Picked Calls – Successfully answered calls.
  • Holded Calls – Calls placed on hold.
  • Routed Calls – Redirected to another line, extension, or department.
  • Recorded Calls – Calls recorded for quality or compliance.
USE OF VOIP CALL ANALYTICS TOOLS/SOFTWARE
  • Call Performance Monitoring – Tracks duration, quality, dropped calls, volume.
  • Quality of Service (QoS) Assessment – Monitors clarity, latency, jitter, packet loss.
  • Cost Management and Billing – Analyzes call costs.
  • User and Department Analytics – Insights into user or department call activity.
  • Call Routing and Handling Insights – Shows call routing and holding details.
  • Historical Data and Trend Analysis – Maintains records over time.
  • Alerts and Notifications – Real-time alerts for call quality or performance drops.
  • Compliance and Recording Management – Tracks recorded calls for legal or regulatory purposes.
REPORTING OF VOIP INCIDENTS
Description of VoIP Incident Categories
  • Poor Call Quality – Unclear, choppy, delayed, or distorted voice communication.
  • Sign-On Issues – Problems logging in or registering VoIP devices/accounts.
  • Slow Load Time – Application or interface responds slowly.
  • Batch Job Errors – Errors during automated processes.
Use of VoIP Incident Reporting Tools/Software
  • Real-Time Incident Logging – Record incidents as they occur.
  • Categorization and Prioritization – Classify incidents by type and priority.
  • Automated Alerts and Notifications – Notify admins when incidents occur.
  • Tracking and Status Management – Monitor incident resolution progress.
  • Analysis and Reporting – Reports to identify trends and recurring issues.
  • Integration with Other Tools – Combine with VoIP monitoring or ITSM systems.
